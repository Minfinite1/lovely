Muhammad

The most influential man who ever lived

Here's a timeline of Muhammad's life:

  • 570 CE - Birth
    Muhammad was born in Mecca, in the Arabian Peninsula. He belonged to the Quraysh tribe, a respected merchant tribe.
  • 576 CE – Death of His Mother
    At age six, Muhammad lost his mother, Amina, leaving him an orphan. He was then raised by his grandfather and later by his uncle, Abu Talib.
  • 578 CE – Death of His Grandfather
    After the death of his grandfather, Muhammad came under the care of his uncle, who provided him with protection and guidance.
  • 595 CE – Marriage to Khadijah
    Muhammad, at 25, married Khadijah, a wealthy widow 15 years his senior. Their marriage was supportive and marked by mutual respect; they had several children together.
  • 610 CE – First Revelation
    At the age of 40, Muhammad received his first revelation from the Angel Gabriel while meditating in the Cave of Hira. This marked the beginning of his prophethood.
  • 613 CE – Public Preaching Begins
    Muhammad began to publicly preach Islam, calling for monotheism and social justice. This led to opposition from the Quraysh leaders.
  • 615 CE – First Migration to Abyssinia
    Facing persecution in Mecca, some of Muhammad’s followers migrated to Abyssinia (modern-day Ethiopia) seeking refuge under the Christian king, Negus.
  • 619 CE – Death of Khadih
    Khadijah passed away, marking a personal loss for Muhammad, who had relied on her support throughout his prophethood.
  • 620 CE – Night Journey and Ascension
    According to Islamic tradition, Muhammad experienced the Isra and Mi'raj, a miraculous night journey to Jerusalem and ascension to the heavens, reinforcing his role as a prophet.
  • 622 CE – Hijra to Medina
    Due to increasing hostility in Mecca, Muhammad and his followers migrated to Medina (then Yathrib), marking the start of the Islamic calendar.
  • 624 CE – Battle of Badr
    Muhammad led his followers in the Battle of Badr, a pivotal victory against the Quraysh, which solidified his position in Medina and bolstered Muslim morale.
  • 625 CE – Battle of Uhud
    The Muslims faced a setback in the Battle of Uhud against the Quraysh. Although they showed bravery, strategic mistakes led to a loss, highlighting the need for unity.
  • 628 CE – Treaty of Hudaybiyyah
    A pivotal peace treaty with the Quraysh allowed Muslims to return to Mecca for pilgrimage, significantly improving the Muslim community's standing.
  • 630 CE – Conquest of Mecca
    Muhammad and his followers peacefully entered Mecca, reclaimed the Kaaba, and established it as the center of Islamic worship, solidifying the faith’s presence.
  • 632 CE – Farewell Pilgrimage
    Muhammad performed his final pilgrimage, delivering the Farewell Sermon, which emphasized equality, justice, and the core tenets of Islam.
  • 632 CE – Death
    Muhammad died in Medina after a brief illness at the age of 63. His death marked the end of his prophetic mission but the beginning of the spread of Islam across the world.
